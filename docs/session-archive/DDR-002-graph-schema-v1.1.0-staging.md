## Decision

DDR-002 fixes the **node / relationship / constraint / index contract** for the SOFIA graph system-of-record. It owns the schema; it does **not** own architecture (DDR-001), feedback-loop governance (DDR-003), or service realization (the SDDs). Decomposed into testable ruling components:

- **Decision.1** — The schema defines the node, relationship, constraint, and index contract for the **Knowledge Graph (KG)**, the **Reasoning Graph (RG)**, and the **Artifact node-family**, co-resident in one Neo4j Enterprise instance (DDR-001 Decision.1).
- **Decision.2** — The KG is the **five core planes** fixed by DDR-001 (Catalog, Environment, Operational, Governance, Standards) **plus the Extension plane**, where cost registers as the first Extension registration (R23) — cost is **not** a sixth core plane.
- **Decision.3** — The RG is **captured reasoning**, cross-graph-linked to the KG, and is **not** a KG plane (carries no plane label).
- **Decision.4** — The **Artifact family** (produced deliverables — solutions, BCDR plans, build sheets) is a node-family *within* the two-graph store — **neither a KG plane nor a third graph** — joined to the KG and RG only by edges.
- **Decision.5** — The schema is bound by a **two-axis provenance model** (`origin_mechanism` × `derivation_class`), a **reversibility-tiered completeness posture** (R25), DB-enforced uniqueness/existence constraints, and a **CI-only conformance-invariant set** with a bound, named exposure window (R27).
- **Decision.6** — This DDR owns the schema contract only; architecture (DDR-001), feedback-loop governance (DDR-003), and service realization (the SDDs) are routed, not authored, here.

## Rationale

This ruling is **deliberation-grounded**: it is the output of the RBT-13 pre-authoring design session, which deliberated the node/edge/constraint shape plane by plane against DDR-001's ratified data architecture and ADR-001/ADR-002's reasoning-and-system-of-record commitments. The design space, the alternatives weighed at each plane, and the disposition of every review finding were worked through in that session; this DDR carries the settled contract.

The completeness posture is deliberately **reversibility-scoped, not a uniform dial** (R25): identity keys, constraints, edge-binding properties, and invariant/traversal-bearing properties are locked now because they are expensive to reverse downstream (DDR-002 gates twelve SDDs and is cited by DDR-003); descriptive and rich application payload stay schema-on-write and SDD-level. Where the design cycle surfaced a mechanism with **no empirical instance yet** — and locking it would over-specify against R25 — the capability is named as a gap (see *Named Gaps*) and re-instated by additive amendment when a real instance arises, rather than carried speculatively. This is the empirical-warrant discipline that governs what is locked versus named.

## 1. Schema grammar & global conventions

**Naming.** Node labels `PascalCase`; relationship types `SCREAMING_SNAKE`; properties `snake_case`; primary keys `<entity>_id`.

**Provenance (origin-neutral group; two-axis).** Every **KG** node carries a provenance group — minimally `origin_mechanism` + `recorded_at` (and `source_record_ref` where applicable); DB-existence-constrained (§7). Provenance is split into **two orthogonal axes**:

- **`origin_mechanism`** (how the node entered — `ingested` external ground truth · `authored` human/SOFIA-authored · `promoted` materialized through the feedback-loop EA gate · `derived` produced by an internal process), always present and invariant-bearing;
- **`derivation_class`** (the nature of the content — `primary` · `distilled` · `aggregated`), present where it carries information.

The two axes form a **total scoping matrix**: `ingested → primary` · `authored → primary` · `derived → distilled | aggregated` · **`promoted → N/A`**. `derivation_class` is **N/A for `promoted`** by design (R25-correct): a promoted node carries `origin_mechanism: promoted` as its provenance-axis fact, and its pre-promotion *content nature* (was the source distilled? aggregated? authored reasoning?) lives **at the source node**, recovered by traversing the provenance chain (§5) — never denormalized onto the promoted node. This de-tangles a process-vs-nature conflation: a distilled `ObservedPattern` and a computed `CapabilityCostEstimate` each become independently queryable on both axes — (`origin_mechanism: derived`, `derivation_class: distilled`) and (`origin_mechanism: derived`, `derivation_class: aggregated`) respectively.

**`source_record_ref`** is the structured, durable reference to the originating external system of record — `{source_system_class, record_id}` (system named by *class*, not a vendor product, per DDR-001's source-named-by-class convention — §Two-Graph Model). Honoring R24 (the graph holds the reference, not the record): **required on `ingested`**; applicable as a source/stream ref on `derived`-from-external (a distilled `ObservedPattern` references its telemetry source); **N/A on `authored`/`promoted`** (provenance is internal lineage).

**`recorded_at`** is the uniform system/record time (when the graph captured the node); domain timestamps (`decided_at`, `first_observed_at`/`last_observed_at`, `computed_at`, `generated_at`) are node-type-specific and never conflated with it.

The `promoted` vs. `ingested` distinction — a **DDR-001 conformance-check #6 obligation** (promoted knowledge must be distinguishable from ingested) — is carried by `origin_mechanism`. RG and Artifact provenance differ (§4, §5).

**Provenance scope.** The provenance *existence constraint* (§7) scopes to: **KG nodes**, the **three authored RG types** (`ReasoningSession`, `ReasoningProgress`, `RejectedAlternative`, all `origin_mechanism: authored`), the **derived, group-carrying `ProvenanceSummary`** (`origin_mechanism: derived`; v1.1.0, §4), and the **Artifact family** (§5). `RejectedAlternative` carries the group as authored content because DDR-001 frames it as ASA-authored and the *primary feedback-loop input* (§Reasoning Graph) — the highest-leverage promotion signal must not carry the weakest provenance. **`Evidence` is the sole surrogate-only RG type:** its provenance is recovered structurally via `SOURCED_FROM` (the atomic capture-unit, §7), not a carried group (§4).

**No vectors (R6) — both halves.** *Negative half:* no node or edge carries vector/embedding properties (documented invariant + conformance check). *Positive half:* the graph-native retrieval substrate that *replaces* vector similarity is the existing structure — the `l1/l2/l3` capability/pattern taxonomy (§2.1), the selection-edge web (§3), and the track-record subgraph (§2.3). DDR-002 **names** these as the R6 substitute and restates the no-vector prohibition as binding; the concrete **retrieval affordance** (graph-traversal query patterns; whether a `SIMILAR_TO` edge is warranted and how it would be populated without embeddings) is **routed to the knowledge-service / solutioning SDDs** (RBT-15, RBT-17), not designed here.

**Classification / no-PHI (R10).** Data classification is gateway-enforced at ingestion; SOFIA stores no PHI *data*. Note the distinction: PHI/PCI *policies* are legitimate Standards knowledge (rules about architectures that handle such data); R10 strips the data, not the rules.

**Plane membership — hybrid (KG-scoped).** Every **KG** node carries a **secondary plane label** (enables cheap label-scoped traversal) **and** the plane is declared in a `PlaneDefinition` registry node (§2.6). Core planes are fixed by DDR-001; the registry is the Extension surface. **RG nodes and Artifact-family nodes carry no KG plane label** — they are not plane citizens; they are reached by cross-graph traversal (§5). This exemption is what lets plane-scoped ground-truth traversals skip reasoning state and produced artifacts structurally.

**Completeness posture (R25) — the tiering that governs every node/edge.** Scope by *reversibility*, not a uniform dial:

- **T1** — identity / structural keys, constraints, edge-binding properties. *Expensive to reverse → complete now.*
- **T2** — invariant- or traversal-bearing properties (filters, discriminators, version pins, status). *Drives behavior → complete now, indexed where it drives traversal.*
- **T3** — descriptive payload. *Cheap to add (schema-on-write) → minimal now, gaps named not padded.*
- **T4** — rich application payload. *SDD-level, not schema.*

**Contested-T2 annotation.** Where a T2 value-set is **POC-asserted** rather than invariant-grounded, it is flagged *(contested)* so a spike-generalization review pass can revisit it. Current contested T2s: `conclusion_type` enum (§4), `GateDecision.gate` enum (§2.4, → RBT-41), `polarity` neutral option (§2.3), `basis_strength` (§5). Invariant-grounded T2s (plane labels, version pins, status discriminators) are not flagged.

**Denormalization boundary (R25, consolidated).** Cross-plane concerns stay in their owning plane, reached by traversal — never duplicated onto a node. R25's "never duplicated" governs **hot-path live-fact duplication**; it is **not** breached by two bounded, justified kinds, named here once so neither reads as a local exception:

- **Point-in-time / retention-boundary snapshots** — `Evidence.fact_summary` + `source_node_version` pins (§4), and the frozen provenance snapshot that preserves a promoted node's lineage past `Evidence` expiry (the `(:Reasoning:ProvenanceSummary)` node, §4/§5; v1.1.0). These capture *what a fact said at a point in time*, precisely because the live source can drift or be deliberately retained-out — a **temporal-fidelity** concern R25 does not govern (its "traverse to the live home" presupposes a live home).
- **Sync-checked edge-mirrors** — `dependency_manifest` (PolicyRule + Condition, §2.5) mirrors `GOVERNED_BY`/`MANDATES` structure to make opaque-logic reads introspectable, kept honest by conformance check #10 (the mirror must match the edges) and its declared-not-undeclared caveat (§7).

## 2. The planes — node schemas

Property tier in parentheses. `provenance (T1)` denotes the provenance group. §2.1–§2.5 are the five core planes fixed by DDR-001; §2.6 is the Extension surface, where cost registers first per R23.

### 2.1 Catalog — the selection graph (versioned ground truth)

- **`(:Catalog:Pattern)`** — `pattern_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `pattern_type` (T2, indexed) · `status` (T2, indexed, versioned) · `effective_from`/`superseded_by` (T2) · `name` (T3) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Catalog:Capability)`** — `capability_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `name` (T3) · `l1_taxonomy`/`l2_taxonomy`/`l3_taxonomy` (T2) · `status` (T2) · `description` (T3) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Catalog:Technology)`** — `technology_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `name` (T3) · `vendor` (T3) · `platform` (T3) · `approval_status` (T2, indexed) · `version_constraint`/`min`/`max` (T2) · `deprecation_date` (T2) · `tier_applicability[]` (T2) · `approved_data_classifications[]` (T2) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Catalog:IacTemplate)`** — `iac_template_id` (T1 PK) · `template_type` (T2) · `name` (T3) · `version` (T1) · `status` (T2) · provenance (T1)

The `l1/l2/l3` taxonomy + the selection edges are the **graph-native retrieval substrate** (R6 positive half, §1) — affordance design → RBT-15 / RBT-17.

*Reference vs. produced.* "Solution documents" as *reference* Catalog content (template/exemplar solutions, library knowledge) are distinct from the **produced** `(:Artifact:Solution)` keystone (§5). A reference solution is Catalog ground truth; a produced solution is an Artifact. They are not the same node, and Catalog never holds the produced ASD.

### 2.2 Environment — the as-running graph (ages via freshness)

- **`(:Environment:DeployedService)`** — `deployed_service_id` (T1 PK) · `service_type` (T3) · `lifecycle_state` (T2, indexed) · `observed_at` (T2) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: ingested` · `derivation_class: primary`, runtime/CMDB source class)
- **`(:Environment:DeploymentEnvironment)`** — `environment_id` (T1 PK) · `name` (T3) · `environment_class` (T2, indexed — production/staging/dev) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Environment:ConfigurationItem)`** — `ci_id` (T1 PK) · `ci_type` (T2, indexed) · `name` (T3) · `observed_at` (T2) · provenance (T1)

*Plane signature:* freshness (`observed_at`) — runtime facts are less authoritative than catalog ground truth; this lets the RG weight environment evidence lower. *Lifecycle distinction:* `DeploymentEnvironment` is the **realized** runtime environment, distinct from a solution's design-time `target_environment` (§5). Environment is DDR-001-sanctioned CMDB-sourced ground truth.

### 2.3 Operational — the track-record graph (durable distillation, not telemetry)

- **`(:Operational:ObservedPattern)`** — `observed_pattern_id` (T1 PK) · `polarity` (T2, indexed — `strength` / `weakness` / optional `neutral` *(contested)*) · `pattern_type` (T2, indexed) · `description` (T3) · `observation_window` (T3) · `confidence` (T2 — **lesson reliability**; see §4 canonical def; per-target observation certainty rides the `OBSERVED_IN` edge, §3) · `first_observed_at`/`last_observed_at` (T2) · `status` (T2, indexed — active/superseded/resolved/**archived**) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: derived` · `derivation_class: distilled`)

*Per R24 / R26.* The KG does **not** mirror transient SoR telemetry. It holds the durable distilled lesson — a recurring operational pattern, of **dual polarity** (a clean track record is as informative as a weakness). **No in-graph TTL.** Raw telemetry stays TTL-bounded in the **external** observability/AIOps SoR; it is not a KG plane and is not mirrored. **Bounded by distinctness.** Distillation is **update-in-place** — the *active* `ObservedPattern` set is bounded by the number of distinct durable lessons (each updated as evidence accrues via `first_observed_at`/`last_observed_at`/`confidence`), **not** a node-per-observation; the never-delete posture is qualified to *never silently delete*, with terminal-state (`resolved`/`superseded`) patterns transitioning to **`archived`** per **DDR-003** policy. *Per v1.1.0 (RBT-43), DDR-002 commits the concrete `archived` `status` value, discharging the v1.0.0 DDR-003 dependency: `archived` is **retention-tier demotion, never hard-deletion** — an archived pattern stays traceable and is reversible if a dormant pattern recurs (R26 A-5 clarification).* The archival **policy** (the terminal-state-plus-dwell trigger, cadence) remains DDR-003's, and the write is a non-EA-gated `derived` Operational-plane write per the **R30 derive/promote authority seam** (Operational is the staging tier; the EA gate sits between it and authoritative knowledge — so this distillation write does not breach ADR-001's SOFIA-never-self-modifies invariant). The distillation step (reading the SoR, generalizing patterns) writes `ObservedPattern` with `origin_mechanism: derived` / `derivation_class: distilled` and a `source_record_ref` to the external telemetry source; the process itself (cadence, generalization criteria, ownership) → **DDR-003 / detection-promotion SDD** (RBT-14). Weakness/strength entry into the KG is **EA-gated** via the feedback loop (§5), not auto-ingested. *This plane aligns to DDR-001 §Two-Graph Model → Operational (durable distilled `ObservedPattern`s, v1.1.0); the upstream serialize condition is cleared (see Pre-Acceptance Conditions).*

### 2.4 Governance — the decision/audit graph (immutable append-only; references, not records)

**Decision supertype + subtypes.** A single shared decision *shape* with two subtype labels, so SDLC gates and the feedback-loop promotion gate share structure without being conflated. **Subtype integrity:** every `Decision` carries **exactly one** subtype label — never bare, never both — enforced as a CI-only invariant (§7; Neo4j cannot enforce label cardinality natively). The **approving-state set** (referenced by the promoted-origin invariant, §7) is `{approved, approved_conditional}`.

- **`(:Governance:Decision)`** *(supertype shape — never instantiated bare; every decision node also carries a subtype label)* — `decision_id` (T1 PK) · `decided_at` (T2) · `approval_token_id` (T3) · provenance (T1). Decided-by via `(Actor)-[:APPROVED]->(Decision)`. **The verdict is not a supertype property:** it rides the `DECIDED_ON {outcome ∈ approved | approved_conditional | rejected}` edge (§3, §5) — a property of *the decision's act on each target* — so a `GateDecision` carries one outcome and a batch `PromotionDecision` carries a per-candidate outcome on each edge, with no single-value supertype field to break on batches and no double-record.
- **`(:Governance:Decision:GateDecision)`** *(SDLC gates)* — adds `gate` (T2, indexed — SDLC gate discriminator; **placeholder `gate_0`/`gate_1`/`gate_2` (contested) → reconcile to enterprise gate taxonomy, RBT-41**) · the standard provenance group with **`origin_mechanism`** as a **live property** (currently **`ingested`** for the external-captured gate, the only grounded case; **`authored` reserved-and-available** for a future SOFIA-as-authoritative-decider state, ADR-001 Position 4, expressible without re-architecture) · **`source_record_ref`** `{source_system_class, record_id}` (the standard structured external reference, per §1) · `all_hard_constraints_passed` (T2) · `rejection_reason` (T3, conditional). `DECIDED_ON {outcome}` → **Solution** (§5).
- **`(:Governance:Decision:PromotionDecision)`** *(feedback-loop EA approval — SOFIA-issued)* — **no `source_record_ref`** (SOFIA is the issuer, not a capturer). `DECIDED_ON {outcome}` → **CandidatePromotion** (§5), **1:many** (a single batch decision may decide multiple candidates — the **per-candidate verdict lives on each `DECIDED_ON {outcome}` edge** — so one decision can approve some, reject others, and conditionally-approve others; `CandidatePromotion.status` separately carries the candidate's own *lifecycle* state, which legitimately diverges from a historical edge verdict). **Verdict precedence:** governance is append-only — a re-decision is a new `PromotionDecision` with a new `DECIDED_ON` edge, so a candidate may carry multiple `DECIDED_ON` edges with differing outcomes. The **governing** verdict is the outcome of the edge whose `PromotionDecision` has the **latest `decided_at`** (no `SUPERSEDES` edge type; re-decision is permitted). Conformance check #15 keys promotion authority off the *governing* approving edge (§7), so a node materialized on a now-superseded approval is detected when the governing verdict has since become a rejection (its un-promotion remediation rides the **retraction shape**, §5 / §7 #21). Batch-eligibility *policy* → DDR-003. **Retraction (v1.1.0, RBT-43):** un-promotion of a promoted KG fact is locked as schema — an **EA-gated reversing `CandidatePromotion` + `RETRACTS` edge** (§5; §7 #21), per DDR-001's EA-gated-mutation principle. The *remedy boundary* — supersession (the default, when wrong-but-replaceable; §6 versioning already supports it) vs. retraction (the exception, when the fact should never have been promoted and has no correct replacement) — is **DDR-003 governance (R31)**, not authored here; DDR-002 owns only the schema shape.

- **`(:Governance:Actor)`** — `actor_id` (T1 PK) · `actor_type` (T2, indexed — `human` / `system`) · `name` (T3) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Governance:Role)`** — `role_id` (T1 PK) · `name` (T3) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Governance:Attestation)`** — `attestation_id` (T1 PK) · `result` (T2, indexed — pass/fail) · `attested_at` (T2) · provenance (T1) *(closes obligation↔satisfaction, §3)*
- **`(:Governance:Condition)`** *(applicability predicate for `approved_conditional` promotions)* — `condition_id` (T1 PK) · `predicate` (T2 — structured, evaluable predicate over solution/Catalog properties; pattern-aligned with `rule_definition`, carrying a declared `dependency_manifest` — covered by check #10) · `created_at` (T2) · provenance (T1). Linked from its `PromotionDecision` (`HAS_CONDITION`) and governs the conditionally-promoted node's applicability **by traversal** (R25 — not duplicated onto the node, beyond the `applicability_state` marker, §5). The knowledge is promoted *with* this condition (not held pre-promotion). A `conditional` node is admitted to a consuming context only if that context **satisfies its `Condition` predicate** (single-condition, check #19; evaluated at retrieval — §7; RBT-15 / RBT-17), which is what lets the same knowledge be reused across qualifying solutions. **Multi-condition → named DDR-003 gap (see *Named Gaps*):** if a fact is conditionally promoted more than once it is structurally **one node reached by multiple `HAS_CONDITION` paths** (forced by `(business_key, version)` uniqueness); the **multi-condition consumption semantics** (disjunctive vs. otherwise) and the **condition-set lifecycle** (activation/retirement/`status`; accrete vs. supersede) are a named gap — DDR-002 adds **no** `Condition.status` field now, resolving them when a real multi-condition case arises. Condition vocabulary → DDR-003.

*Key postures:* for `GateDecision` in the current build, the external entity is the system of record and the node is a **captured reference**, not SOFIA's authoritative record (`source_record_ref` carries the external reference; `origin_mechanism: ingested`). **Forward-compatibility:** a future state in which SOFIA *itself* makes the gate decision authoritatively (ADR-001 Position 4) is accommodated by `origin_mechanism: authored` (approving `Actor` = `system`) — an existing field taking its other live value, no new property and no re-architecture; check #17 already exempts `authored` from `source_record_ref`, so a SOFIA-authoritative gate correctly carries none. The intermediate case — SOFIA *issues* a gate decision but an external system holds the authoritative record — has no current instance; if it ever arises it is a one-field additive amendment, not locked now. For `PromotionDecision`, SOFIA *is* the issuing authority over the feedback loop, so there is no external record to point at — the de-conflation keeps the SDLC-gate semantics from leaking onto the promotion gate and vice-versa. `Actor` generalizes the POC's "User" so any decider — human, SOFIA, or another external system — has a structural home. The per-action **disposition-event stream is excluded** (transient → orchestration/observability SoR, per R24). `Actor`/`Role` are governance-participating identities, **not** an IAM mirror. Immutable append-only: a re-decision is a new node.

### 2.5 Standards — the governance-of-knowledge graph (versioned + change-impact)

- **`(:Standards:Standard)`** — `standard_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `name` (T3) · `authority` (T3) · `status` (T2) · provenance (T1) *(the referenceable authority — "per policy 1234")*
- **`(:Standards:PolicyRule)`** — `policy_rule_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `rule_name` (T3) · `domain` (T2, indexed) · `status` (T2, indexed, versioned) · `superseded_by` (T2) · `enforcement_level` (T2 — hard/soft) · `enforced_at_gate` (T2) · `statement` (T3 — natural-language requirement) · `rule_definition` (T3 — opaque declarative IF/THEN evaluation logic, the validator consumes it; the graph never traverses into it) · `dependency_manifest` (T2 — declared, introspectable list of the Catalog entity-types/labels the rule reads; the evaluation logic stays opaque, the manifest does not) · provenance (T1)
- **`(:Standards:ComplianceControl)`** — `control_id` (T1 PK) · `framework` (T2, indexed — HIPAA/SOC2/PCI as *data*) · `control_ref` (T3) · `control_name` (T3) · provenance (T1)

*Decision (executable rule logic on the node):* the executable rule logic rides the `PolicyRule` node as opaque `rule_definition` (KG-as-policy-SoR, matching DDR-001's served-from-graph model); the constraint-validator evaluates it. **`rule_definition`↔edge sync invariant:** every Catalog entity-type in a rule's declared `dependency_manifest` MUST appear as a `GOVERNED_BY` / `MANDATES` target for that rule — the opaque evaluation logic may not hide a dependency the graph can't see, and because the manifest is introspectable the invariant is mechanically checkable. **Honesty caveat:** check #10 enforces *declared-but-wired* — every manifest entry is edge-backed — **not** *declared-complete* against the still-opaque `rule_definition`. An author who omits a real dependency from the manifest passes #10 while the omission persists. Closing that (a runtime check that a rule reads *only* manifest-declared entities; or extraction from a parseable rule form) is **validator behavior routed to RBT-22**, not schema — DDR-002 does not re-open the rule-logic-on-node opacity decision (the same posture R27 takes: name the exposure, track the mechanization). Documented invariant + conformance check (§7). **`GOVERNED_BY`'s role:** the stored, traversable record of *entity-triggered* applicability (a standing Catalog fact — any solution using this entity inherits this rule), keeping governance graph-queryable; the obligation-closure mechanism (entity-triggered traversal ∪ condition-triggered compute, with validator-logic closure) lives in §3. A standard is broader than rules — it can `DEFINE` a `PolicyRule`, `PRESCRIBE` a `Technology`/`Pattern`, etc. **The `Process` node type and its edges are deferred (→ RBT-40)**; `PRESCRIBES` keeps its non-Process targets and loses the Process target until Process lands. Mandates resolve in two ways: **structurally** (validator reads the solution graph — prohibitions, "uses the vault") or **by attestation** (process performed / evidence produced — `Attestation`, §2.4).

### 2.6 Extension mechanism + cost plane (the R23 exemplar — first Extension registration)

**Mechanism:**
- **`(:Extension:PlaneDefinition)`** — `plane_id` (T1 PK) · `plane_name` (T3) · `version` (T1) · `status` (T2, indexed) · `attaches_to` (T2 — list of core labels the plane may enrich) · `property_schema` (T2 — **structured, validator-consumable** schema descriptor: node-label → property → type/constraints, which schema-on-write enforces) · provenance (T1)

The registry is self-describing (query `PlaneDefinition` nodes to enumerate registered planes). `attaches_to` and `property_schema` are **declarations** (properties), not edges-to-labels — a simplification of the POC's edge-modeled `DEFINES`/`ATTACHES_TO`. Schema-on-write validates extension nodes against their `PlaneDefinition`; instance-edges are bounded to the `attaches_to` labels. Extension nodes carry the plane's secondary label (the hybrid, §1). **`attaches_to`-exists invariant:** every label in a `PlaneDefinition.attaches_to` must reference an existing core label (CI-only, §7). The cost plane below is the **worked validation exemplar** — standing it up exercises the registration path end-to-end, so the Extension mechanism is *tested by construction*, not merely asserted (the R23 first-registration benefit).

**Cost plane — first registration:** `PlaneDefinition{plane_id: "cost", attaches_to: [Capability, Technology]}`.
- **`(:Cost:RateCard)`** — `rate_card_id` (T1 PK) · `version` (T1) · `effective_from`/`effective_to` (T2) · `status` (T2, indexed) · `rates` (T3, unit→cost payload) · provenance (T1). *Never-deleted; superseded only.*
- **`(:Cost:CostFactor)`** — `cost_factor_id` (T1 PK) · `factor_scope` (T2, indexed — technology/capability) · `factor_type` (T2, indexed) · `amount`/`unit` (T3) · `version` (T1) · `status` (T2) · provenance (T1). *Reference input.*
- **`(:Cost:CapabilityCostEstimate)`** *(immutable, basis-pinned, as-of-decision record; NOT a refreshable cache)* — `estimate_id` (T1 PK) · `aggregate_cost` (T2 — **derived at creation, then frozen**, never hand-set) · `rate_card_version_ref` / `cost_factor_version_ref` / `capability_version_ref` (T2 — the pinned as-of basis) · `confidence` (T2) · `computed_at` (T2 — the as-of timestamp) · `decision_ref` (T2 — the option/decision this estimate informed) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: derived` · `derivation_class: aggregated`). **Append-only and immutable; never claims currency** — current/hypothetical cost is **compute-on-read** (Tier-1/2 traversal + DTO, RBT-25). Persisted for decision-traceability (which option's cost drove a choice, immutably as-of) and estimate-vs-actual variance (the actuals side is external/Environment — RBT-25). **Retention:** as-of records are **justifiably permanent**, not the R24 transient-telemetry anti-pattern — they are *decision-paced and low-volume by design* (one per option per decision, not event-stream-paced) and *needed permanently* (decision-traceability + variance both require the record forever; archiving one would break its variance computation). This is structurally unlike Operational's archivable `resolved`/`superseded` tail (R26): a cost as-of record has no decision-closed terminal state. If volume ever surfaces a query-performance concern at scale, the **volume/query-management affordance** (partitioning, indexing, cold-tiering of decisions whose variance window has closed — *not* deletion) → RBT-25 / DDR-003.

*Cost is the first temporally-mixed plane:* `RateCard`/`CostFactor` versioned reference, `CapabilityCostEstimate` an **immutable as-of-decision record** (not refreshable; current cost compute-on-read). Tier-1/2 traversal + the `Tier1CostEstimateResult` DTO → RBT-25.

## 3. KG relationships — edge grammar + catalog

**Edge grammar:**
- **Direction & naming** — `SCREAMING_SNAKE` verb phrases in the natural semantic direction; Neo4j traverses both ways, so direction is for readability, not access. One edge per relationship — no redundant inverses.
- **Edge properties** — attributes belonging to the *relationship*, not either node.
- **Edge provenance** — asserted-fact edges (ingested, e.g. `APPROVED_OPTION_FOR`) carry provenance (`origin_mechanism`, §1); structural edges do not.
- **Re-bind discipline** — each edge *type* declares a supersession behavior, **governed by the point-in-time principle**: edges that record a *point-in-time fact* (a solution's use of a version, evidence citations, decision references) are **`rebind:pinned`** — they never re-point, because re-pointing would falsify the historical record; edges expressing a standing *current* pointer (current-recommended, selection-preference) are **`rebind:current`** — they re-point to the new version on supersession so the hot path stays current. Because every potentially-unbounded edge is pinned by correctness necessity, the `rebind:current` re-point set is **bounded-degree by construction**; the residual — any `rebind:current` type that proved unbounded — would fall back to identity-indirection for that type (not adopted now). Declared per edge type in the catalog below.
- **Cardinality** — not constrainable in Neo4j schema → documented invariant + conformance check.
- **Temporal edges** — relationships whose *validity* is time-bound carry `effective_from`/`effective_to` + `status`; structural edges do not.
- **Confidence** — confidence lives **on the surface whose certainty it describes**: *edge* confidence = certainty of an observed relationship (Environment as-running edges; the Operational `OBSERVED_IN` per-target certainty); *node* confidence = reliability of a derived/reasoned thing (`ObservedPattern` lesson reliability, `CapabilityCostEstimate`, `ReasoningProgress`, `Evidence`). KG *structural* edges are authoritative by default (no confidence). Canonical definition + per-surface composition rule in §4.

**Edge catalog (five core-plane sub-graphs + Cost/Extension):**

*Selection (Catalog) — `rebind:current`:* `(Pattern)-[:REQUIRES_CAPABILITY {required, tier_conditional, tier_threshold}]->(Capability)` · `(Technology)-[:APPROVED_OPTION_FOR {conditional, justification}]->(Capability)` *(temporal)* · `(Pattern)-[:PREFERRED_OVER]->(Pattern)` · `(Technology)-[:REPLACED_BY]->(Technology)` *(temporal)* · `(IacTemplate)-[:IMPLEMENTS]->(Pattern)`

*As-running (Environment, confidence-bearing):* `(DeployedService)-[:RUNS {confidence}]->(Technology)` · `(DeployedService)-[:DEPLOYED_IN {confidence}]->(DeploymentEnvironment)` · `(DeployedService)-[:REALIZES {confidence}]->(Capability)` · `(ConfigurationItem)-[:PART_OF {confidence}]->(DeployedService)`

*Track-record (Operational):* `(ObservedPattern)-[:OBSERVED_IN {confidence}]->(Technology | Pattern | Capability | DeploymentEnvironment)` *(edge `confidence` = per-target observation certainty, distinct from the node's lesson-reliability `confidence` — composed at rollup)*

*Governance-of-knowledge (Standards):* `(Standard)-[:DEFINES]->(PolicyRule)` · `(Standard)-[:PRESCRIBES]->(Technology | Pattern)` *(Process target deferred → RBT-40)* · `(PolicyRule)-[:MAPS_TO]->(ComplianceControl)` · `(PolicyRule)-[:MANDATES]->(Technology)` · `(Pattern | Technology | Capability)-[:GOVERNED_BY]->(PolicyRule)` *(the stored, traversable record of **entity-triggered** applicability — any solution using this entity inherits this rule. Condition-triggered applicability is computed from `rule_definition`; the two combine in the obligation-closure model below.)*

*Decision/audit (Governance):* `(Actor)-[:HAS_ROLE]->(Role)` *(temporal)* · `(Actor)-[:APPROVED {role, approved_at}]->(Decision)` *(targets either subtype via the supertype)* · `(GateDecision)-[:REVIEWED {hash}]->(Artifact)` · `(Attestation)-[:SATISFIES]->(PolicyRule)` · `(Attestation)-[:BY]->(Actor)` · `(Attestation)-[:EVIDENCED_BY]->(Artifact)`. *(`(Attestation)-[:FOR_PROCESS]->(Process)` deferred entirely → RBT-40.)*

*Cost (Extension):* `(CapabilityCostEstimate)-[:FOR_CAPABILITY]->(Capability)` *(cardinality 1)* · `(CapabilityCostEstimate)-[:HAS_COST_FACTOR]->(CostFactor)` · `(CostFactor)-[:FOR_TECHNOLOGY]->(Technology)` · `(CapabilityCostEstimate)-[:PRICED_BY]->(RateCard)` *(`rebind:pinned`)*

*Solution-centric and promotion edges are deferred to the keystone (§5).*

**Obligation↔satisfaction model (hybrid).** Standards hold the *obligation*; Governance holds the *attestation*; the solution sits in the middle. The **validator computes the obligation set** — *entity-triggered* obligations reached by traversing `GOVERNED_BY`/`MANDATES` from the solution's `USES`/`FOLLOWS` entities, **plus** *condition-triggered* obligations from evaluating `rule_definition` conditions against the solution — then joins each obligation to its satisfaction: validator-structural (prohibitions, structural requirements) or attestation-evidenced (`Attestation`). The **closure is validator logic, not a single graph traversal** (the graph stores the associations, the rule logic, and the attestations). A missing satisfaction surfaces as a compliance gap (an RG risk inference), not silence. Closure logic + manifest consumption → constraint-validator (RBT-22).

## 4. The Reasoning Graph — node schemas

**Label reconciliation** (align to ADR-002 §2.6 / ADR-001 §2.2 vocabulary, retiring POC names): `Inference` → `ReasoningProgress`; `Hypothesis` → `RejectedAlternative`; `Evidence` and `ReasoningSession` kept. Per **ADR-002 §2.6**, the rich `ReasoningProgress` *fields* are SDD-level; DDR-002 owns only the schema contract. RG nodes carry **no KG plane label** (§1).

- **`(:Reasoning:ReasoningSession)`** — `session_id` (T1 PK) · `lifecycle_state` (T2 — **AOE-owned; value-set deferred to the AOE SDD, RBT-16, with a named placeholder; DDR-001 likewise leaves it unspecified**) · `created_at` (T2) · provenance (T1). *(No 1:1 `solution_ref` — the session→solution link is the `PRODUCED` edge (§5), **0..\***: a session may explore multiple candidate solutions or conclude none. Consistent with DDR-001 §Reasoning Graph, which pins one session per run, not one solution per session.)*
- **`(:Reasoning:ReasoningProgress)`** — `progress_id` (T1 PK) · `conclusion_type` (T2, indexed — PatternMatch / TechnologySelection / GapConclusion / OverrideFlag / RiskSignal / ComplianceEvaluation; **(contested); reconciliation below**) · `confidence` (T2) · `overridden_by_human` (T2) · `created_at` (T2) · provenance (T1). *= the typed conclusion (lean reading: no separate container). Rich conclusion/rationale → SDD.*
- **`(:Reasoning:Evidence)`** — `evidence_id` (T1 PK) · `fact_summary` (T2 — denormalized snapshot) · `confidence` (T2, inherited) · `weight` (T2) · `source_node_version` (T2 — version pin) · `observed_at` (T2)
- **`(:Reasoning:RejectedAlternative)`** — `rejected_id` (T1 PK) · `candidate_type` (T2, indexed) · `rejection_reason` (T3) · `score_delta` (T2) · `human_accepted` (T2) · `human_accepted_at` (T2) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: authored`; §1)
- **`(:Reasoning:ProvenanceSummary)`** *(v1.1.0, RBT-43 — the realized provenance-survival snapshot; R25 N-3 / R27 amendment 1b)* — `provenance_summary_id` (T1 PK) · `frozen_fact_summaries` (T2 — the denormalized `fact_summary` content of the **set** of originating `Evidence` the promotion's provenance chain spans, frozen at materialization so it survives `Evidence` expiry) · `frozen_source_version_pins` (T2 — the corresponding `Evidence.source_node_version` pins, likewise frozen) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: derived`). The **frozen lineage** a post-expiry audit needs is preserved two ways: the *expiring* content (the `Evidence`-set summaries + their source-version pins) is **denormalized onto this node** as a retention-boundary snapshot (R25 category-(i), §1 — a temporal-fidelity snapshot, **not** hot-path duplication, because the live `Evidence` is deliberately retained-out); the *durable* anchors (the originating `CandidatePromotion` and, through it, the governing `PromotionDecision`) stay **live-traversable** via `MATERIALIZES_PROVENANCE_OF` (§5) and the existing append-only Governance edges (which never expire). It carries **no KG plane label** (not ground truth) and is a `derived` RG-region node. *Materialized **at promotion** (§5, §6), so build-before-`Evidence`-expiry holds by construction.* **The exact internal structuring of the multi-`Evidence` snapshot, plus the reverse-lookup index/affordance + query patterns + whether the affordance warrants its own CI invariant, remain a named gap → RBT-15** (see *Named Gaps*).

**Confidence — canonical definition.** Confidence is a `[0.0, 1.0]` scalar living **on the surface whose certainty it describes**: **node** confidence = reliability of a derived/reasoned thing — on `Evidence` *source authority × freshness* (authoritative Catalog above aging Environment, decayed by `observed_at`), on `ObservedPattern` the *lesson reliability*, on `CapabilityCostEstimate` the estimate reliability, on `ReasoningProgress` the *rolled-up conclusion*; **edge** confidence = certainty of an observed relationship — Environment as-running edges, and `OBSERVED_IN` *per-target observation certainty*. Thus **Environment = edge-only; Operational = node (lesson reliability) + edge (per-target certainty)**, the two composed at rollup. **Inheritance/rollup rule:** `Evidence.confidence` inherits from the `SOURCED_FROM` KG node's authority class at snapshot time; `ReasoningProgress.confidence` is a (SDD-defined) rollup over its `SUPPORTED_BY` Evidence — DDR-002 fixes that the rollup exists and is monotone (a conclusion is no more confident than its strongest evidence path), the function itself → SDD.

**`conclusion_type` ↔ correction-surface ↔ DDR-001 reconciliation.** The six `conclusion_type` values are the *typed-conclusion* enum. They map onto the seven correction-surfaces of §5 (technology, pattern, capability, integration, risk, compliance, cost) — `integration` and `cost` surface as `TechnologySelection`/`GapConclusion` variants rather than distinct conclusion types — and align with DDR-001's signal/action classes (no contradiction: DDR-001's classes are coarser). The enum is confirmed aligned; the mapping table is authored here so the three vocabularies are reconciled in one place. *(The enum itself remains contested pending the spike-generalization pass.)*

*Write authority (**ADR-002 §2.6** invariant):* ASA authors `ReasoningProgress`; AOE owns `ReasoningSession` lifecycle only; writes route via knowledge-service. Mechanization → RBT-33.
*RG-internal edges:* `(ReasoningSession)-[:CONTAINS]->(ReasoningProgress)` · `(ReasoningProgress)-[:SUPPORTED_BY]->(Evidence)` · `(ReasoningProgress)-[:REJECTED]->(RejectedAlternative)` · `(ReasoningProgress)-[:LED_TO]->(ReasoningProgress)`.
*Point-in-time fidelity:* `fact_summary` + `source_node_version` make the audit trail immune to later KG drift.

**RG provenance posture.** Provenance is present across the RG, but expressed by node kind:
- `ReasoningSession` and `ReasoningProgress` are *authored reasoning state* → they carry the provenance group (provenance = the authoring session/agent, ASA per §2.6).
- `RejectedAlternative` is **authored content** → it carries the provenance group (`origin_mechanism: authored`), because DDR-001 §Reasoning Graph frames it as ASA-authored and the *primary feedback-loop input*; the highest-leverage promotion signal must not carry the weakest provenance.
- `Evidence` is the **sole** *surrogate-only* RG type — the sole derived RG type that omits the group; its provenance is **structural** — it pins `source_node_version` + `observed_at` and links `SOURCED_FROM` the authoritative KG node it snapshots (§5), written as an **atomic capture-unit** (`Evidence` + `SOURCED_FROM` in one gateway transaction, §7) so the surrogate is **sound by gateway contract** (CI-enforced via check #14, exposure named — not a DB guarantee).
- `ProvenanceSummary` *(v1.1.0)* is a **`derived` RG type that carries the group** (`origin_mechanism: derived`) — it is **not** surrogate-only; it is a materialized derivation whose own origin is recorded, distinct from the `Evidence` structural surrogate it preserves. It therefore joins the §7 existence-constraint scope.

So the provenance *existence constraint* (§7) scopes to **KG nodes + the three authored RG types (`ReasoningSession`, `ReasoningProgress`, `RejectedAlternative`) + `ProvenanceSummary` (derived, group-carrying) + the Artifact family**; `Evidence` remains the **sole** surrogate-only type. **The surrogate's enforceability gap is named, not hidden (R27):** the cardinality invariants that *make* the `Evidence` surrogate sound — including the **atomic capture-unit** — are CI-only (§7); Neo4j cannot express them as constraints, so they are documented and mechanized at RBT-33, sequenced ahead of RBT-15 so the gateway is built against an enforced contract.

**Accountability boundary.** RG content is captured *judgment*, not held to the KG determinism invariant. The EA gate (`PromotionDecision`) is the **accountable-consolidation control** — it converts a judgment-derived proposal into an *accountable, ratified, authoritative* KG fact (per ADR-001 §2.5's "EA-gated consolidation into encoded logic"). It restores **accountability and authoritativeness, not determinism**: determinism is a separate, never-lost property of the gap/override *derivation* (a structural function of graph facts — DDR-001 §Two-Graph Model, cross-cutting KG invariants), independent of how any fact entered. Promoted facts are therefore **in-scope** for that derivation by design (the encoding-density-growth feature — a promoted override becomes sanctioned and future deterministic reasoning reads it) and remain **distinguishable** via `origin_mechanism: promoted`. A promoted-origin KG node must still trace to an **approving** `PromotionDecision` (§7) — that ties it to an *accountable* decision. The *policy* of how the derivation weights promoted-vs-ingested facts, and the *health* of judgment-consolidation over time → DDR-003 / reasoning-machinery (RBT-14 / RBT-27).

## 5. Cross-graph linkage + the feedback loop

**Ownership line (R8 / R22) — what DDR-002 owns vs. cites in this section.** DDR-002 **owns** the *schema contract*: the `(:Artifact:Solution)` and `CandidatePromotion` node shapes, the cross-graph and promotion edge grammar, and the *structural* invariants (append-only terminal status, provenance-chain traceability). DDR-002 **cites, and does not re-author:** the promotion *mechanics / data-path* → DDR-001; the *diagnosis policy / EA approval criteria / thresholds / cadence* → DDR-003 (RBT-14); the *workflow* → SDD. The Refine/Request-new/Correct-scope framing and the "EA-gate is a diagnosis" characterization below are stated for schema motivation only — their governance is DDR-003's, their data-path is DDR-001's.

**The Artifact family — a node-family within the two-graph store.** `(:Artifact …)` is a **distinct node-family**, *not* a third graph or third citizen-class peer to the KG and RG: the store is the two-graph model (KG + RG, R8 / DDR-001), and Artifacts are nodes *within* it that bridge produced deliverables to the reasoning that made them and the catalog they draw on. It is **neither a KG plane nor RG** — the produced-deliverable family (solutions, BCDR plans, build sheets). It carries its own PK, its own provenance group (`origin_mechanism: authored`/`derived`), and its own uniqueness, and it carries **no KG plane label** (so plane-scoped ground-truth traversals skip produced artifacts). It joins KG and RG only by edges. This resolves the question of whether `Solution` is a Catalog node or an RG node: it is **neither** — it is an Artifact node-family member (R8 governs the produced-solution treatment; DDR-001 §Versioning & Temporal Model and §Hybrid Persistence Model — solution dual-home).

**Evidence edges (RG → KG):** `(Evidence)-[:SOURCED_FROM]->(KG node)` *(`rebind:pinned`; confidence inherits here — authoritative Catalog scores above aging Environment)* · `(RejectedAlternative)-[:WOULD_HAVE_USED]->(KG node)`. *(`DREW_FROM`/`TRAVERSED` deferred — the considered-set is reconstructable from Evidence + RejectedAlternative.)*

**The Solution keystone:**
- **`(:Artifact:Solution)`** — `artifact_id` (T1 PK) · `artifact_type` (T2 — `solution`) · `version` (T1) · `lifecycle_state` (T2, indexed — FSM: proposed→architected→gated→approved→operational) · `content_hash` (T2 — tamper-detection) · `snapshot_ref` (T2 — dual-home pointer) · `target_environment` (T2 — design-time intent, drawn from the `environment_class` controlled vocabulary) · `created_at` (T2) · provenance (T1, `origin_mechanism: authored`). `(:Artifact)` is the general produced-deliverable family (solutions, BCDR plans, build sheets).

*Bridge edges:* `(ReasoningSession)-[:PRODUCED]->(Solution)` *(**0..\*** — a session may produce multiple candidate options or none)* · `(Solution)-[:USES]->(Technology)` · `(Solution)-[:FOLLOWS]->(Pattern)` · `(GateDecision)-[:DECIDED_ON {outcome}]->(Solution)` · `(Solution)-[:HAS_ARTIFACT]->(Artifact)` *(Solution → component sub-artifacts)*. `target_environment` is a **controlled-vocabulary string** (the `environment_class` value-set) resolved by string-match, **not** an FK-edge to `DeploymentEnvironment`; the realized environment arrives later via `DeployedService`. Dual-home mechanics → §6.

**Selection among produced candidates.** When a session `PRODUCED`s multiple candidate `Solution`s, **selection is the approving `(GateDecision)-[:DECIDED_ON {outcome: approved}]->(Solution)` edge** plus `lifecycle_state` advancement (`gated → approved`) — **no `selected` flag** (a redundant boolean would re-create the divergence the edge-borne verdict avoids). Before gating there is no recorded selection (selection is a governance act); the ASA's pre-gate *primary-candidate* pick is solutioning-runtime state → **RBT-17**. Non-selected candidates remain `proposed`/`architected` and are retained as the explored option-set. **Boundary — not-selected `Solution` vs `RejectedAlternative`:** a not-selected `Solution` is an **artifact-level** complete candidate deliverable that wasn't gated; a `RejectedAlternative` (§4) is a **decision-step-level** discarded conclusion *within* the reasoning trail. They are different granularities and not interchangeable; "why a candidate wasn't chosen" is reasoning reachable via the gate decision — no new edge.

**CandidatePromotion + feedback loop (R22)** — *schema contract owned here; mechanics cited to DDR-001 per the ownership line above:*

- **`(:Reasoning:CandidatePromotion)`** — `candidate_id` (T1 PK) · `promotion_type` (T2, indexed) · `proposed_change` (T3 — opaque) · `requested_item` (T3) + `requested_item_kind` (T2 — technology/pattern/capability/integration) + `target_ref` (T3) *(for request-new gaps)* · `basis_strength` (T2 — *contested*) · `confidence` (T2) · `status` (T2, indexed — proposed / under_review / approved / **approved_conditional** / rejected / promoted) *(the candidate's **lifecycle** state; the decision's per-candidate verdict rides the `DECIDED_ON {outcome}` edge — and the two legitimately diverge)* · `created_at` (T2) · provenance (T1). **Append-only**; terminal `status` (`promoted` / `rejected {reason}`) is the durable evaluation outcome — declines are explainable and gaps don't re-surface. A single `PromotionDecision` may decide this candidate as part of a **batch** (1:many `DECIDED_ON {outcome}`); `approved_conditional` carries an applicability `Condition` (§2.4). **Reversing (retraction) variant (v1.1.0, RBT-43):** a retraction is the **same `CandidatePromotion` node carrying `promotion_type: retraction`** plus a `RETRACTS` edge (§Promotion edges) — it goes through the **same EA gate** (`DECIDED_ON` by an approving `PromotionDecision`) as a forward promotion, so un-promotion is accountable, not a bare delete (§7 #21). It reuses the **shared `status` lifecycle** read uniformly as *the candidate's proposed change*: terminal `status: promoted` denotes the retraction was **materialized** (the proposed un-promotion applied — the `RETRACTS` edge written and the target read-excluded), not that anything was forward-promoted; no new `status` value is added. The remedy-boundary *governance* (supersession vs. retraction) is DDR-003 (R31); the schema shape is owned here.

- **Provenance-survival guarantee (form locked v1.1.0, RBT-43; retrieval affordance → RBT-15).** A **durable terminal-`promoted`** node's provenance must remain **recoverable after its originating `Evidence` is retained-out**, preserved as **frozen traversable structure** (R25-correct — not an opaque blob; post-expiry audit and derivation-nature recovery stay a traversal, not a parse), and **materialized before** the `Evidence` it summarizes is removed. This is the schema-relevant guarantee DDR-002 commits (grounded in DDR-001 §Versioning & Temporal Model — summary-on-evidence-expiry; the provenance chain's expiry-survival, below, leans on it). **The concrete form is now locked (R25 N-3 / R27 amendment 1b):** a dedicated **`(:Reasoning:ProvenanceSummary)`** node (§4, `origin_mechanism: derived`) — **not** a frozen subgraph — that denormalizes the expiring `Evidence` content (`frozen_fact_summaries` + `frozen_source_version_pins`) as a retention-boundary snapshot and stays bound to the durable promotion record by `(:ProvenanceSummary)-[:MATERIALIZES_PROVENANCE_OF]->(CandidatePromotion)`. **Materialization timing is locked at-promotion**, so the build-before-`Evidence`-expiry ordering holds **by construction** (it is an invariant of the promotion act, §7 #20 — not a sweep that races retention). **The residual gap → RBT-15** is the *reverse-lookup index/affordance* (the query patterns over `ProvenanceSummary`, and whether that affordance warrants its own CI invariant), to finalize with the retention policy and the first real instance. Framed as a *retention-boundary snapshot*, not hot-path denormalization (R25 clarification, §1).

**`applicability_state` marker — on promoted KG nodes.** Every node materialized via `PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE` carries `applicability_state` (T2, indexed — `unconditional` (default) / `conditional`), **distinct from** the version-lifecycle `status` (a node can be the active version *and* `conditional`). It is the structural key the §7 read-discipline invariant filters on: active ground-truth traversals admit a `conditional` node only where the consuming context satisfies its `Condition` (§2.4, check #19). A `conditional` node is retained and fully traceable — never hard-deleted. **Per-version + supersession carry-forward:** `applicability_state` is **per-version**, established by that version's own promotion decision. Supersession (§6) must **preserve** an EA-set conditional scope — a successor to a `conditional` node is **not** consumed `unconditional` absent an explicit EA `PromotionDecision` re-scoping it (a silent default-to-`unconditional` would over-admit past an EA-set `Condition`, a failure check #19 cannot catch because the successor would no longer be marked `conditional`). DDR-002 commits this integrity expectation; the carry-forward *mechanism* on supersession (including any cross-origin case) and whether it also warrants a CI invariant → **RBT-15 / RBT-33**. **Two-surface applicability:** applicability is constrained on two distinct surfaces — **Catalog eligibility** fields (`tier_applicability[]`, `approved_data_classifications[]`) on **ingested** `Technology` (§2.1), and `applicability_state: conditional` + `Condition` on **promoted** knowledge — and **both bind**. Their composition into a single consumption decision ("may I use this here?") is the consuming SDD's responsibility → **RBT-15 / RBT-17**; DDR-002 names the two surfaces, not a unification mechanism.

*Promotion edges:* `(CandidatePromotion)-[:PROPOSED_FROM]->(ReasoningProgress | Evidence | ObservedPattern | RejectedAlternative)` · `(PromotionDecision)-[:DECIDED_ON {outcome}]->(CandidatePromotion)` *(the feedback-loop EA gate — distinct subtype; **1:many** for batch decisions with per-candidate `outcome`)* · `(PromotionDecision)-[:HAS_CONDITION]->(Condition)` *(for `approved_conditional`)* · `(CandidatePromotion)-[:PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE]->(KG node)` *(materialized node stamped `origin_mechanism: promoted` + `applicability_state`)* · `(ProvenanceSummary)-[:MATERIALIZES_PROVENANCE_OF]->(CandidatePromotion)` *(`rebind:pinned`; v1.1.0 — the at-promotion provenance snapshot's binding to its durable promotion record, §7 #20)* · `(CandidatePromotion)-[:RETRACTS]->(KG node)` *(`rebind:pinned`; v1.1.0 — the reversing-CandidatePromotion's un-promotion target; EA-gated, §7 #21)*.

*Structural & read-discipline invariants:*
- **Proposal-visibility (dissolved to DDR-001 + gateway).** `CandidatePromotion` must not appear in ground-truth synthesis traversals until EA-approved. This is **owned by DDR-001** as the proposal-visibility invariant + conformance check #4 (`SOFIA never self-modifies the KG`; promotions excluded from ground-truth traversal until approved). DDR-002 supplies the *structural aid* — `CandidatePromotion` carries no KG plane label, so plane-scoped traversals skip it — but names honestly that full enforcement is **gateway-enforced read-discipline** (the sole-owner knowledge-service scopes ground-truth reads), **not** a pure structural guarantee. Lands in the R27 CI-only set (§7); cites DDR-001 check #4, no DDR-001 amendment needed.
- **Provenance chain.** A promoted node traces back through `PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE` → `CandidatePromotion` → `PROPOSED_FROM` → `ReasoningProgress`/`Evidence`/`RejectedAlternative` → `SOURCED_FROM` → original facts; `origin_mechanism: promoted` makes it distinguishable from `ingested` (DDR-001 check #6). Its pre-promotion *derivation nature* is recovered by this same traversal to the source, not denormalized. **Expiry survival:** when the originating `Evidence` expires, the chain is preserved by the **provenance-survival guarantee** (above) — frozen traversable structure via the `ProvenanceSummary` node (form locked v1.1.0); the *retrieval affordance* over it is the residual named gap → RBT-15. **Approving-decision invariant:** a promoted node must trace, via `PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE` ← `CandidatePromotion` ← the **governing** approving `DECIDED_ON` edge — the edge whose `PromotionDecision` has the **latest `decided_at`**, with `outcome ∈ {approved, approved_conditional}` — to a `PromotionDecision` (CI-only, §7 #15). Keying off the *governing* edge (not any approving edge) means a node whose governing verdict has since flipped to `rejected` is detected even if a stale earlier `approved` edge exists; its un-promotion remediation is the **locked retraction shape** (the EA-gated reversing `CandidatePromotion` + `RETRACTS` edge, §7 #21; remedy-boundary governance → DDR-003 R31).
- **Retracted-node read-discipline (v1.1.0).** A KG node carrying an inbound `RETRACTS` edge from an **EA-approved** reversing `CandidatePromotion` is excluded from ground-truth synthesis traversals — **gateway-enforced read-discipline keyed on the EA-approved `RETRACTS` edge**, parallel to #9 (proposal-visibility) and #19 (conditional-consumption); no denormalized `retracted` status marker (the edge is the structural fact). The retracted node is **retained and fully traceable** (durable rationale on the audit trail per R31) — never hard-deleted.

**Generalized correction mechanism** *(schema motivation; governance → DDR-003).* Every `conclusion_type` is a human-correction surface (technology, pattern, capability, integration, risk, compliance, cost — reconciled to the `conclusion_type` enum in §4), in three flavors: **Refine** (override to an existing option → context-scoped, evidence-backed preference signal on the selection edge, no pairwise cycles; the *runtime preference-landing* → solutioning SDD, RBT-17), **Request-new** (gap → human evaluation/onboarding; never auto-created), **Correct-scope** (false risk / mis-scoped rule → adjust existing ground truth). The capture machinery (`overridden_by_human`, `human_accepted`, `GapConclusion`, `RejectedAlternative`, `CandidatePromotion`, EA-gate, `PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE`) is general across surfaces. The "override is a signal the EA *diagnoses*" framing is governance characterization owned by DDR-003, not authored here.

## 6. Versioning & temporal model

**Per-posture spectrum** (each plane fit to its nature): Catalog/Standards/RateCard **version** (supersession), Environment **ages** (`observed_at`), Operational **distills** (status-managed), Governance **accretes** (immutable append-only), RG **pins** (point-in-time), Solutions/Artifacts **dual-home**.

**Supersession — Option A (scoped to versioned-ground-truth types: Catalog, Standards, RateCard, CostFactor, PlaneDefinition).** A node carries `version` + `effective_from`/`effective_to` + `status` + `superseded_by`; superseding creates a new retained node and marks the old superseded. **Atomicity:** supersession is a **single atomic transaction** — create new version node, mark old `superseded`, and re-point the `rebind:current` edges to the new node — so a traversal never observes a half-superseded state with two active versions. `rebind:pinned` audit/evidence/consumption edges are untouched (they pin to their specific version node). **The re-point is O(degree) but bounded by construction:** the point-in-time principle (§3) forces every potentially-unbounded edge (solution-consumption, evidence, decision-reference) to be `rebind:pinned`, so the `rebind:current` set is limited to standing current-pointer edges (low-degree); the assumption is stated, and the residual — any `rebind:current` type that proved unbounded — falls back to identity-indirection for that type (not adopted now). The edge re-pointing is the **gateway's** responsibility (knowledge-service, sole writer — consistent with DDR-001's gateway pattern, R8 boundary; cited, not re-authored). Never-delete via retained versions; as-of via effective-dating. Chosen over a blanket identity+version-node pattern to keep the selection-graph hot path hop-free (versioning is rare; traversal is constant).

**Version-pinning.** `Evidence.source_node_version` + `CapabilityCostEstimate`'s pinned basis (`rate_card_version_ref` / `cost_factor_version_ref` / `capability_version_ref`) reference specific retained version nodes (`rebind:pinned`); with `Evidence.fact_summary` that yields point-in-time fidelity. The immutable as-of cost record is the cost-plane instance of this point-in-time discipline.

**Durable promotion records.** A terminal-`promoted` `CandidatePromotion` is **expiry-exempt** (durable); the **provenance-survival guarantee** (§5) holds that when bounded RG retention expires the originating `Evidence`, the promoted node's traceability is preserved as **frozen traversable structure** (not a blob), materialized before the `Evidence` is removed, keeping post-expiry audit graph-native (schematizing DDR-001's summary-on-evidence-expiry — §Versioning & Temporal Model). **Form + materialization timing/ordering are locked (v1.1.0, RBT-43):** a dedicated `(:Reasoning:ProvenanceSummary)` node (§4), materialized **at promotion** via `MATERIALIZES_PROVENANCE_OF` so build-before-expiry holds by construction (§7 #20). The **retention policy** (the `Evidence`-expiry windows the §5.1 retention classes set) → DDR-003 (RBT-14), and the **retrieval index/affordance** over `ProvenanceSummary` → RBT-15 (named gap — see *Named Gaps*).

**Dual-home (Artifact/Solution).** The graph node holds metadata, edges, `content_hash`, `snapshot_ref`; immutable content lives in the external snapshot store (Firestore per R9, routed to DDR-001); the hash is tamper-detection (snapshot hash must match `content_hash`). A new version is a new node + new snapshot; the old pair is retained.

**Uniqueness under versioning.** PKs unique on `(business_key, version)`; "exactly one active version per business_key" is a documented invariant + conformance check.

## 7. Constraints, indexes & conformance

**DB-enforced (Neo4j Enterprise) — generating rules:**
- **Uniqueness** on every PK; `(business_key, version)` for the versioned-ground-truth types.
- **Existence constraints** on the provenance group (`origin_mechanism`, `recorded_at`) on every **KG** node, the **three authored RG types** (`ReasoningSession`, `ReasoningProgress`, `RejectedAlternative`), the **derived `ProvenanceSummary`** (v1.1.0; `origin_mechanism: derived`), and the **Artifact family** (see §4 for the RG-provenance posture) — plus each node's T1 required props. (The Enterprise *edition* committed in ADR-002 — edition per **R3** — buys existence-constraint capability.)
- **Indexes** on the flagged T2 traversal/filter properties only (lean — no speculative indexing); plane secondary-labels are inherently indexed. *The index set is **provisional**, revisitable once the SDDs surface real query patterns.* **Reverse cross-graph lookup:** `SOURCED_FROM` is stored RG→KG, so the reverse direction (KG-node → citing-`Evidence`, "what reasoning cited this fact?") needs an index/affordance — named here; the concrete index lands with the retrieval patterns in the knowledge-service SDD (RBT-15).

**Conformance-checked invariant set — CI-only (Neo4j cannot express → documented + CI; mechanization → RBT-33; enforcement posture per R27).** Neo4j existence/uniqueness constraints cannot express these; they are honestly named as CI-only with a stated exposure window (below), uniform under the R27 posture:

1. **RG-provenance edge invariants (safety-critical tier, sequenced ahead of RBT-15):** every `(:Reasoning:Evidence)` carries a **mandatory** `SOURCED_FROM` edge to a provenance-bearing KG node; every `(:Reasoning:RejectedAlternative)` is reached via **exactly one** parent `REJECTED` edge — justified on **per-conclusion** grounds: a `RejectedAlternative` carries per-conclusion rejection rationale (`rejection_reason`/`score_delta`/`human_accepted`), so it *is* the rejection-by-one-conclusion; the same option rejected by two conclusions is two nodes the feedback loop aggregates by their shared `WOULD_HAVE_USED` target (better for recurrence detection than a shared node). `Evidence` **not** sourced from a provenance-bearing KG node is **prohibited** (`Evidence` is KG-sourced by definition and check #14 enforces it). These cardinality invariants are what make the §4 structural provenance-surrogate sound.
2. **no vector properties** (R6).
3. **relationship cardinality** (e.g. `FOR_CAPABILITY` cardinality 1).
4. **one active version per business_key.**
5. **aggregate-derivation** (`CapabilityCostEstimate.aggregate_cost` derived-at-creation-then-frozen, never hand-set).
6. **schema-on-write** (extension nodes validate vs. `PlaneDefinition`; edges bounded to `attaches_to`).
7. **ADR-002 §2.6 write-authority** (ASA authors `ReasoningProgress`; AOE session-lifecycle only).
8. **never-delete** (versioned types supersede; atomic re-bind bounded by the point-in-time rebind assignment).
9. **proposal-visibility / read-discipline:** `CandidatePromotion` excluded from ground-truth synthesis traversal until EA-approved — gateway-enforced read-discipline + structural label-skip aid; defers to DDR-001 check #4.
10. **`rule_definition`↔edge sync:** every Catalog entity-type in a **`PolicyRule`'s or `Condition`'s** declared `dependency_manifest` appears as a `GOVERNED_BY`/`MANDATES` target. **Caveat:** this enforces *declared-but-wired*, not *declared-complete* against the opaque logic; the completeness check (a rule reads only manifest-declared entities) is validator behavior → RBT-22.
11. **provenance distinguishability (DDR-001 check #6):** `promoted` distinguishable from `ingested` via `origin_mechanism`.
12. **tamper-detection** (`Artifact.content_hash` matches snapshot hash).
13. **no-PHI** (classification, gateway-enforced, R10).
14. **atomic capture-unit:** an `Evidence` node and its `SOURCED_FROM` edge are written in a single gateway transaction — the structural provenance surrogate is **sound by gateway contract, CI-enforced, exposure named** (not a DB guarantee) at the sole-writer choke-point (joins #1; safety-critical tier).
15. **promoted-origin → governing approving decision:** every `origin_mechanism: promoted` KG node traces, via `PROMOTES_TO_KNOWLEDGE` ← `CandidatePromotion` ← the **governing `DECIDED_ON` edge** — the edge whose `PromotionDecision` has the **latest `decided_at`**, with `outcome ∈ {approved, approved_conditional}` — to a `PromotionDecision`. Keyed off the *governing* (not any) approving edge, so a node whose governing verdict has flipped to `rejected` is caught despite a stale earlier `approved`; correct for single and batch decisions alike.
16. **exactly-one-subtype-per-Decision:** every `Decision` carries exactly one of `{GateDecision, PromotionDecision}` — never bare, never both.
17. **ingested ⇒ source_record_ref:** every `origin_mechanism: ingested` node carries a `source_record_ref`; **every `derivation_class: distilled` node** also carries a `source_record_ref` to its external source (scoped to `distilled` — `aggregated` derives from internal version-pins, no external ref). Aligns §1's "applicable on distilled" with §7 enforcement.
18. **attaches_to-exists:** every label in a `PlaneDefinition.attaches_to` references an existing core label.
19. **conditional-consumption read-discipline:** an `applicability_state: conditional` node is admitted to a consuming context only if that context **satisfies its `Condition` predicate** (single-condition; retrieval-enforced, RBT-15 / RBT-17) — parallel to #9's proposal-visibility read-discipline; gateway-enforced at retrieval. The blocking safety control: conditional knowledge is never consumed out-of-scope. *(Multi-condition consumption semantics — named gap, §2.4 / Named Gaps.)*
20. **at-promotion ProvenanceSummary materialization (v1.1.0, RBT-43):** every durable terminal-`promoted` `CandidatePromotion` carries a `(:Reasoning:ProvenanceSummary)` written via `MATERIALIZES_PROVENANCE_OF` **at promotion time** — before any originating `Evidence` can expire, so the build-before-expiry ordering holds **by construction** (a gateway-behavioral materialization check). Makes the §5 provenance-survival guarantee enforceable rather than aspirational. *(Follow tier — enforced with the RBT-15 gateway, not ahead of it; it guards post-expiry audit survival, not facts-entering-ground-truth.)*
21. **retraction-gated (v1.1.0, RBT-43):** a `RETRACTS` edge is valid only from a `CandidatePromotion` (`promotion_type: retraction`) that traces — via `DECIDED_ON` — to an **approving** `PromotionDecision` (`outcome ∈ {approved, approved_conditional}`), with durable rationale on the audit trail. The **un-promotion mirror of #15**: un-promotion of authoritative ground truth is EA-gated, never a bare delete. The retracted node's exclusion from ground-truth reads is the gateway-enforced read-discipline keyed on this EA-approved edge (§5; parallel to #9/#19). *(Safety-critical tier — guards ground-truth integrity against unauthorized mutation; mechanized as a 1b gateway-behavioral contract per R28.)*

**Named exposure window + sequencing (R27).** Until RBT-33 mechanizes them, this CI-only set (21) is review-enforced (three-hat / authoring time), not CI-enforced — an honest gap, tracked. Sequencing is **tiered**, not uniform-after-#1. **Criterion:** an invariant is *precede-RBT-15* if the gateway operating without it enforced would, from its first writes/reads, let a safety-or-integrity-critical violation enter authoritative ground truth or synthesis reads that is hard to detect or reverse — unapproved/self-modified KG facts, wrong-consumption of non-ground-truth (proposal/conditional) as ground truth, provenance-broken or PHI-bearing writes, or unauthorized writes. **Safety-critical tier (ahead of RBT-15):** #1 + #14 (provenance soundness), #7 (write-authority), #11 + #17 (provenance stamping), #15 (no unapproved/superseded-verdict promotion), **#21 (no unauthorized un-promotion — the #15 mirror)**, #9 (proposal-visibility), #19 (conditional read-discipline), #16 (Decision well-formedness), #13 (no-PHI). **Follow tier (uniform RBT-33 timeline):** #2, #3, #4, #5, #6, #8, #10, #12, #18, **#20 (at-promotion ProvenanceSummary materialization — gateway-behavioral, enforced with RBT-15)**. **Authority split:** DDR-002 **binds** (a) the safety-critical **classification** — *which* integrity properties guard against unapproved/self-modified facts entering authoritative ground truth — and (b) the **precede-gateway rule** (the safety-critical set is enforceable before RBT-15 authors the gateway). **RBT-33 finalizes the *mechanization*** (CI-job mapping, within-tier ordering, mechanics) **subject to** that binding — it does not downgrade a safety-critical classification or let a safety-critical invariant slip the gateway gate; **the two v1.1.0 additions (#20, #21) extend RBT-33's conformance set, their CI-job mapping tracked there** (#21 a 1b gateway-behavioral contract per R28). This realizes R27's "RBT-33 sequenced ahead of RBT-15" with the tier (not only #1) ahead.
