### 5b. EA empty emission — a new failure mode, roster evidence

Characterization: this is **variance-to-zero** — a draw-level degenerate completion (immediate empty array), not a hat defect and not content variance. It is hat-independent, input-independent (same bytes both ways), and at the observed rate (~3 of 11 SDD-scale hat-draws) it is common enough to matter. Impact demonstrated concretely: run-009's ledger carries **no EA-altitude finding at all** — an entire altitude silently absent from a single-run view, invisible unless you hold the sibling draw. This is the strongest single datum yet for the queued union-over-runs operating-mode question.

Detectability: a compliant empty emission is **structurally implausible** under the ratified 2-POSITIVE floor — a hat that genuinely finds no defects still owes its strongest survived attacks, so `[]` (or any emission below the POSITIVE floor) is malformed-by-calibration, not a legitimate null result. That makes it mechanically detectable and re-drawable — the reviewer-side analog of the arbiter's content retry. **Flagged as a runner question for the prep-tooling ticket + the queued operating-mode deliberation; not designed here.**

### 5d. Cost shape — prompt-caching flag for the prep-tooling ticket
Run-009 totals: **1,297,750 input / 10,062 output tokens**, 170s wall, single pass, one document. Shape: reviewers 4 × ~99k in (396k, 31%); **arbiter 11 × ~82k = 901k in (69%)**.
